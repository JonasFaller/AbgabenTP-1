MAIN CHARACTERS

Cathy Ames

A moral monster, the most evil character in the novel. Cathy acts out of a perverse love of debasement, destruction, and control. As a young girl, she murders her parents by arson and then starts a life of prostitution. She later marries and then shoots Adam Trask, abandoning her newborn twin sons in order to return to prostitution. After murdering the brothel owner, Faye, Cathy becomes the madam of the brothel, using drugs to control and manipulate her whores. She takes photographs of powerful men involved in sex acts in order to blackmail them. Aron’s discovery that Cathy is his mother breaks him and leads to his death. Cathy represents Eve in the Cain and Abel story of the novel, introducing sin and evil into the world. She commits suicide after lasting Aron’s response to her.

Adam Trask

The son of Cyrus Trask and the father of Aron and Cal. Adam is a goodhearted but useless man, and his innocence leads him to fall in love with the novel’s most evil character, Cathy Ames. In the novel’s retelling of the biblical story of Cain and Abel, Adam plays the Abel role in the first generation of the Trask family; in the second generation, he plays the Adam role.

Charles Trask

The son of Cyrus Trask and the half-brother of Adam. Charles is a violent, manipulative man who works his father’s farm and build up a large fortune. Although Charles is deeply jealous of his brother, he also needs Adam and misses him terribly when he is not at home. Charles plays the Cain role in the first generation of the Trasks. He is one of the only characters able of inspiring fear in Cathy Ames.

Cyrus Trask
The patriarch of the Trask family and the father of Adam and Charles. Cyrus lies so convincingly about his military heroics during the Civil War that the government appoints him to a powerful position in the Army administration. In fact, Cyrus was wounded in the very first hour of his battlefield experience in the Civil War and lost his leg to amputation. Cyrus leaves his fortune of more than $100,000 to his sons.

Mrs. Trask
The first wife of Cyrus Trask and the mother of Adam. Cyrus’s wife, whose name we do not learn, is a deeply religous woman. Mrs. Trask commits suicide.

Alice Trask
Cyrus’s second wife and the mother of Charles. Alice is a quiet woman who almost never shows emotion. One day, however, Adam catches her smiling mysteriously to herself when she thinks no one is watching. Alice dies while Adam is away in the Army.

Aron Trask
The son of Adam and Cathy and the twin brother of Cal. Aron is a goodhearted, trusting boy with deep, innate morality. Aron plays the Abel role in the second generation of the Trask family. When Cal reveals to Aron that their mother, Cathy, is a prostitute, Aron is so angry that he leaves Stanford and joins the army, and soon dies in World War I.

Caleb Trask
The son of Adam and Cathy and the twin brother of Aron. Cal is a manipulative, stormy boy who is very jealous of his more likable brother, Aron. Cal struggles throughout the second half of the novel to control his temptations and to lead a moral life. Cal plays the Cain role in the second generation of the Trask family, indirectly killing Aron by revealing to Aron that their mother is a prostitute, which leads Aron to join the army and die in World War I.

Samuel Hamilton
The patriarch of the Hamilton family. Samuel is a happy, self-educated Irishman who moves his family to the Salinas Valley in California. He is never a rich man, but he is well respected in the community.

Lee
Adam Trask’s cook and housekeeper, an educated man whose parents emigrated to America from China.Throughout the novel, Lee serves as a stabilizing force in the Trask household.

Abra Bacon
The daughter of the corrupt county supervisor in Salinas. Abra, who is as goodhearted as Cathy is evil, offers mercy and common sense to the Trask family.

Mr. Edwards
A man who runs a prostitution ring throughout Massachusetts and Connecticut. Mr. Edwards has a highly moral wife and a pair of sons. After employing Cathy as a prostitute, Mr. Edwards falls in love with her. After discovering her involvement in the murder of her parents, he beats her nearly to death, and she crawls away to the nearest farm—that owned by Charles and Adam Trask.
